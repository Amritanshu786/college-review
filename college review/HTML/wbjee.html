West Bengal Joint entrance Examination


OVERVIEW

West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board conducts West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination (WBJEE) for admission to different Undergraduate Courses in Engineering & Technology, Pharmacy and Architecture in Universities, Government Colleges as well as Self Financed Institutes in the State of West Bengal

Exam Name West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination
Exam Category Undergraduate
Exam Level State
Exam Mode Offline(Pen-Paper)

ABOUT

 PAPERS

Paper Subject(s)
Paper 1 Mathematics
paper 2 Physics and Chemistry

TYPE


Subjects Scope

Mathematics(paper-I)

Physics+Chemistry(Paper-II)

 For seeking admission in Engineering/Technology/Architecture/Pharmacy courses

PATTERN


The exam will be a pen-and-paper based examination

All the questions will be Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ)

There will be three categories of questions in each subject

Answers are to be marked (bubbled) by using only a blue/black ball point pen on the OMR Answer Sheet


QUESTION TYPES



Only one option is correct

Correct option will yield 1 (one) mark

Incorrect option will yield -¼ (negative ¼) marks

For any combination of more than one option, the said answer will be treated as incorrect and will yield -¼ (negative ¼) marks

Not attempting the question will fetch zero marks




Only one option is correct

Correct option will yield 2 (two) mark

Incorrect option will yield -½ (negative ½) marks

For any combination of more than one option, the said answer will be treated as incorrect and will yield -½ (negative ½) marks

Not attempting the question will fetch zero marks




One or more options are correct

Marking all correct options only will yield 2 (two) marks

For any combination of answers containing one or more incorrect options, the said answer will be treated as incorrect and it will yield zero (0) mark

For partially correct answers, i.e. when all correct options are not marked and also no incorrect option is marked, marks awarded = 2 X (no of correct options marked) / (total no of actually correct options)

Not attempting the question will fetch zero marks

FOR ADMISSIONS



Candidate must have passed the Qualifying Examination (10+2 examination) with Physics and Mathematics along with any one of Chemistry / Biotechnology / Biology / Computer Science / Computer Application / Technical Vocational Subject as compulsory subjects

Candidate must have passed these three subjects individually

Candidate must have obtained at least 45% marks (40% for ST, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B, PwD) in these three subjects taken together

Candidate must also have passed English in the Qualifying Examination with at least 30% marks

Candidates must satisfy additional eligibility requirements for admission to Jadavpur University, read more here

For Marine Engineering course, candidates must secure a minimum of 60% marks in Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics taken together and a minimum of 50% marks in English
